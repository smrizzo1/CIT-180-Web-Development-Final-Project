Who doesn't enjoy the sight of wildflowers bobbing in a gentle breeze?

They brighten our day with their lively colors. They inspire poetry and song. They invite us to wander through fields on a warm spring day, basking in the sunlight with them.

Not only are meadows full of native flowers, trees and shrubs beautiful to human eyes, they are a "feast" to the eyes of our native bees and butterflies, as well. Sadly, though, these pollinators are finding less and less to feed on.







Why is planting native flowers and shrubs important?


"Pollinator habitat is shrinking. As roads and developments have replaced meadows and wildlands, pollinators have lost feeding and nesting sites. Remaining patches of prairie and meadow have become more disconnected. That makes it harder for pollinators to reach new breeding sites or find better habitat."1 This means not only fewer resources for pollinators, but also fewer fruits, vegetables, and other crops that rely on our buzzy, fuzzy, and winged friends for reproduction - and that humans rely on as their own food sources. Try to imagine a life without blueberries, tomatos, apples, almonds, cherries, nutmeg, vanilla, strawberries, peaches, peppermint, and so many more. Yes, humans would survive, but without as much pleasure to the palette!2

Additionally, non-native plants can sometimes quickly take over a habitat and become invasive. They can then "crowd out" native plants, not only reducing food sources for pollinators, but also bringing viruses, fungi and bacteria which can "spread from non-native to native pollinators."1

NEWS

From the Xerces Society: Early Signs of Hope as Monarch Butterflies Return to California Overwintering Site (10/21/2021)

"Migratory western monarchs are being reported at their overwintering sites in coastal California in greater numbers than last year, with hundreds at some sites and thousands at others, giving hope for the struggling population. These reports are particularly welcome after the population reached an all-time low of 1,914 butterflies last year..."

Infrastructure bill is a win for bees and butterflies, including monarchs (11/15/2021)

"Today president Biden signed the $1 trillion bipartisan Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act. ...The act authorizes the U.S. Department of Transportation to make $2 million in grants each year over the next five years for states ($10 million total), Native American tribes, and federal agencies to carry out activities that benefit pollinators and monarch butterflies along the roads and highways they oversee..."
