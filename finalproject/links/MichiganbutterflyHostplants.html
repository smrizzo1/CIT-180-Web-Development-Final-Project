Michigan Butterfly Host Plants

These native plants provide food for our Michigan butterflies. Use the table below to select plants for your garden to help our flighy friends! Why do we want to do this? Per the The National Wildlife Federation,1
  1. Many butterflies and native flowering plants in local geographic regions have co-evolved and are therefore reliant on each other for not only reproduction, but for survival.
  2. Native plants "grow" the caterpillars of our favorite butterflies, so planting what they eat will ensure there will be adults fluttering around your garden all summer.
  3. Finally, adult butterflies need nectar to feed, so "try to plant so that when one plant stops blooming, another begins." Not only will this help the butterflies, it will keep your garden looking pretty all season!
